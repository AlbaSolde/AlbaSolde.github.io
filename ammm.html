Algorithmic Methods for Mathematical Methods

Development of an interactive graphic API for the optimization of error probability calculations in digital communications

Crime is soaring in Gotham City, and Batman needs help to keep every street crossing under surveillance. This project addresses the problem of optimally placing and scheduling surveillance cameras across the city to guarantee continuous coverage while minimizing installation and operational costs under strict autonomy and activation constraints.

The problem was formally modeled as an Integer Linear Programming (ILP) formulation and solved using CPLEX to obtain optimal solutions for small and medium-sized instances. Given the combinatorial complexity of the scenario, several heuristic approaches were also designed and implemented, including a greedy constructive algorithm, a local search procedure, and a GRASP metaheuristic.

The proposed heuristics were evaluated on randomly generated Gotham-like city instances and compared against the ILP model in terms of solution quality and execution time. Results show that greedy and GRASP-based methods achieve near-optimal solutions while scaling efficiently to larger instances, providing a practical and cost-effective way to help Batman keep Gotham safe.
